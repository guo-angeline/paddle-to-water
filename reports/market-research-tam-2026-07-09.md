# TAM Analysis: Paddle to Water

**Date:** 2026-07-09 · **Method:** 5-angle web research (participation data, global market, subscription comparables, local commerce, Bay Area sizing) + adversarial verification of the six load-bearing claims. Every headline number survived a refute-attempt pass unless flagged.

---

## Headline answer

| Layer | Subscription TAM | Local commerce TAM | Ads/affiliate TAM | Combined TAM |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Global** | $2.0–2.6B/yr *(low confidence)* | $200–400M/yr *(low confidence)* | ~$200–400M/yr *(low confidence)* | **~$2.5–3.5B/yr** |
| **US** | $0.7–1.1B/yr | $50–125M/yr | $80–200M/yr | **~$1.0–1.4B/yr** |
| **NorCal (current 142-spot footprint)** | ~$42M/yr | $2–5M/yr | ~$3–5M/yr | **~$45–50M/yr** |
| **SF Bay Area (9-county)** | ~$25M/yr | ~$1.5–3M/yr | ~$2M/yr | **~$27–30M/yr** |

- **SAM** (NorCal core paddlers reachable by this product + bookable local commerce): **~$10–13M/yr**
- **SOM** (realistic 3-year obtainable at current model and traffic trajectory): **~$30–100K/yr**

The honest framing: the US TAM is a real ~$1B+ if you define it as "every paddler pays $35/yr" — but the practical ceiling, anchored on what category winners actually capture (AllTrails, Surfline), is a **$20–50M ARR business for a US-wide paddle-conditions winner**, and a **$25–50K/yr subscription business for a Bay-Area-only product**, before local commerce. This confirms the ROADMAP's instinct that PaddlePass is "real but thin alone" regionally: expansion (geographic or revenue-model) is what unlocks TAM, not price.

## 2. Subscription TAM (PaddlePass)

**Willingness-to-pay comparables (all verified against primary pages):**

| Product | Price/yr | Paying subs | Base | Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Surfline Premium | **$119.99** (Apr 2025; was $99) | Last public: 40K in *2012*; "multiples" higher by 2020 | 3M+ monthly audience | unknown |
| AllTrails+ | **$35.99** (Peak tier $79.99) | 1M confirmed (2021); ~2.1M / ~$73M ARR *estimate-only* | 65M+ registered | ~3% of registered |
| onX Hunt | $34.99–99.99 | "over 1M" | — | — |
| Fishbrain Pro | $74.99 | 200K+ (2022) | 15M registered | ~1.3–2% |
| Strava | $79.99 | ~3M (est.) | 150M+ | ~2% |
| Windy.com Premium | $34.99 | — | — | — |
| Freemium median (RevenueCat, 30K+ apps) | — | — | — | **~2.1–2.2% download→paid** |

Two consistent patterns: outdoor freemium converts **2–3% of registered users**, and paddle-adjacent WTP clusters at **$35–120/yr**. The ROADMAP's $35 price and 7% conversion assumption is optimistic on conversion (7% is hard-paywall territory; freemium reality is 2–3%) but $35 is exactly where AllTrails+, onX Premium, and Windy landed.

**Top-down:**
- US: 30M paddlers × $35 = **$1.05B**; core-only at Surfline pricing: 6–7M × $120 = $0.7–0.85B → **US subscription TAM $0.7–1.1B/yr**
- Global: 60–75M paddlers (soft) × $35 → **$2.0–2.6B/yr** (low confidence)
- Bay Area: 725K × $35 ≈ **$25M**; NorCal: 1.2M × $35 ≈ **$42M**

**Bottom-up (what's actually capturable):**
- SAM: NorCal core paddlers (the only people who plausibly pay for conditions): 240K × $35 ≈ **$8.4M/yr**, plus Bay-Area-core-only view: 145K × $35 ≈ $5M
- SOM: reach 20–30% of Bay Area core as registered users over ~3 yrs (~30–45K) × 2–3% paid × $35 ≈ **$21–47K/yr**. At today's traffic (~14 new users/day, 286 total) the near-term number is the ROADMAP's own ~$7K.
- Cross-check via AllTrails scaling: paddling is ~52% of US hiking participation (30M vs ~58M); AllTrails-equivalent execution over paddle implies a **$20–50M ARR ceiling for a US-national category winner** — that's the credible "what this becomes if it wins everywhere" number, not $1B.

## 3. Local commerce / lead-gen TAM

The ROADMAP hypothesis ("may out-earn the subscription") checks out directionally at the regional layer:

- US kayak/canoe/paddleboat **rental** industry: **$105.3M revenue, 2,285 businesses** (IBISWorld 2024, confirmed). Rental-only and likely undercounts SUP-first outfitters; adding lessons/tours (posted Bay Area rates: rentals $30–42/hr, lessons $60–125) puts US paddle-experiences GMV plausibly at **$250–500M**.
- Take-rate benchmarks: OTAs (Viator, GetYourGuide) take **20–30%** of booking value for demand generation; booking-SaaS (FareHarbor, Peek) takes ~6%. A spot app doing lead-gen prices between those bands.
- **US TAM: $250–500M GMV × 20–25% ≈ $50–125M/yr** (full intermediation). Near-term addressable is smaller: only ~33% of experiences book online today (Arival/Phocuswright 2025), → $17–40M.
- **NorCal:** ≥9 identified SF Bay operators + 16 Tahoe outfitters + Sacramento/Delta → est. 40–60 operators, est. $8–20M paddle GMV → **$2–5M/yr TAM**. Realistic SOM as referral fees on bookings the app demonstrably drives: **$10–60K/yr** (e.g., 2–5K bookings × $60 basket × 6–20%). Note: this monetizes *free* users and casual paddlers (the 53% who rent), which the subscription cannot.
- Global: experiences GMV is $271B (2025) but the paddle sliver is unmeasurable with public data; ~$200–400M/yr is an order-of-magnitude guess.

## 4. Ads / affiliate TAM

Soft ceiling, included for completeness: global SUP **equipment** market is ~$2.0–2.3B (IMARC/Coherent, 2024–26; ~10% CAGR — genuinely fast-growing), US share ~$0.8–1B, plus US kayak gear. Affiliate at 4–6% on app-influenced purchases plus niche-audience advertising ($2–5/user/yr) gives a **US TAM of ~$80–200M/yr**, but capture requires AllTrails-scale traffic. Regionally negligible near-term (~$2M theoretical, effectively $0 obtainable at current MAU).

## 6. What this means for the roadmap

1. **The Bay-Area-only subscription is a lifestyle-scale business** (~$25–50K/yr obtainable). The data confirms the ROADMAP's "thin alone" judgment — retention proof matters more than monetization right now, which is what the current plan already says.
2. **Local commerce roughly doubles the regional obtainable revenue and monetizes the majority-renter casual segment** — the 53%-don't-own-a-board stat is the strongest new argument for sequencing rentals/lessons lead-gen sooner rather than later once retention reads out.
3. **The expansion story is what makes the TAM real.** Every 10× of geography ≈ 10× of TAM at roughly constant product cost: NorCal ($45M) → US West Coast (~$150M) → US ($1B+). The AllTrails-scaled ceiling for a national winner is $20–50M ARR — a real company, and the moat argument (per-spot local judgment) has to be rebuilt region by region, which cuts both ways: slower to scale, harder to copy.
4. **Assumptions most likely to be wrong:** (a) Bay Area participation = national 9.8% rate (no CA-specific survey exists; income and coastline suggest it's a floor); (b) NorCal = ⅓ of CA population share (pure scaling); (c) 7% conversion in the ROADMAP model — benchmark reality is 2–3% freemium; the $7K back-of-envelope should probably read ~$2–3K.
